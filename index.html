§01

TL;DR

In one screen

PPO/GRPO-style trust regions treat every token the same. But in autoregressive generation, early tokens matter more and drift accumulates along the prefix. CPPO reallocates the divergence budget accordingly — a drop-in token mask, no new loss term.

  • Problem. A uniform threshold $D_t \le \delta$ is loose on high-impact early tokens and over-tight on late ones, with no memory of how far the prefix has already drifted.
  • Fix. A position weight $w_t$ tightens early positions; a cumulative prefix budget $\delta_b$ caps the weighted prefix average. Together they give a provably tighter improvement bound.
  • Result. Best AIME24/25/26 Avg@16 on all four Qwen3 settings; +5.56 over the best baseline on 30B-A3B-Base, and stable where others collapse.

§03

Why Uniform Fails

$$|\Delta(\mu,\pi)| \le \sum_{t \lt T}\lambda_t\, u_t, \qquad \lambda_t = 4\xi\,\bar{\ell}\,(T-t)$$
!

The asymmetry

The multiplier $\lambda_t \propto (T-t)$ grows with the remaining horizon: an early-token deviation reshapes every later token's conditioning, so its error compounds over the whole suffix. A flat $D_t \le \delta$ ignores this — under-penalizing early deviations and over-restricting late exploration.

Uniform Threshold

  • Same $\delta$ at every position
  • Loose on early, high-impact tokens
  • No memory of accumulated drift

CPPO (Ours)

  • $w_t$ tightens early tokens
  • Relaxes shorter-horizon late tokens
  • $\delta_b$ caps cumulative prefix drift

Method

CPPO
01

Position-Weighted Threshold

A decreasing linear schedule yields a position-dependent threshold $D_t \le \delta/w_t$ — strict early, relaxed late:

$$w_t = 1 - \frac{1-w_{\min}}{T-1}(t-1),\qquad w_t \in [w_{\min}, 1]$$
02

Cumulative Prefix Budget

An effective threshold caps the weighted prefix average, tightening as drift accumulates:

$$w_t D_t \le c_t^{\text{CPPO}} := \min\{\delta,\; \delta + \delta_b W_{t-1} - S_{t-1}\}$$
03

Token Mask & Surrogate

The two constraints fold into one feasibility test $I_t$; corrective updates are never blocked, and the mask plugs straight into the PPO/GRPO ratio–advantage objective:

$$M_t^{\text{CPPO}} = \mathbb{1}\!\left(\hat{A}_t(\rho_t-1)\le 0 \;\vee\; I_t\right),\quad I_t : w_t D_t \le \delta \;\wedge\; S_t \le \delta + \delta_b W_{t-1}$$
$$\mathcal{L}^{\text{CPPO}}_{\mu}(\pi) = \mathbb{E}_{\mu}\!\left[\sum_{t=1}^{T} M_t^{\text{CPPO}}\,\rho_t\,\hat{A}_t\right]$$

Reuses the same per-token divergence as DPPO — no extra loss terms, no new estimator.
